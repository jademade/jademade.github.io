a hollographic pyramid
for the german pavillion

"Less is more" is a communication strategy designed to revitalize the German Pavilion, designed by Mies van der Rohe for the 1929 International Exhibition in Barcelona, which today goes virtually unnoticed. Based on a thorough analysis of the building and its history, we created a campaign centered around a portable pyramid structure, capable of projecting holographic videos from any smart phone. It is a story from a bygone era told to new generations through today's lens.

the campaign

Thought for an audience interested in design and art but not necessarily familiar with archictecture, the campaign consisted of distributing the pyramid at trendy cafés in Barcelona.

The pyramid would be "served" along with the customer's order. Its simple yet elegant packaging contained a short message with instructions to scan a QR code leading to a video animation introducing the German Pavillion.
